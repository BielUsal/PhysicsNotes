Get $\vec{E}$ and $\vec{D}$

We start by the usual:

$$\rho_{P} = - \vec{\nabla} \cdot \vec{P} = \frac{\frac{1}{r^{2}}d}{dr}(r^{2}P_{r)} = 0$$

$$\sigma_{P}= \vec{P} \cdot \vec{n}$$
But we have two regions, 

$$\sigma_{P}(2a) = \frac{k}{4a^{2}})$$
$$\sigma_{P}(a) = -\frac{k}{a^2}$$

So, we have that:

When $0<r<a$

$$\vec{E} = 0$$

When $a<r<2a$

$$\vec{E} = -\frac{k}{\epsilon_{0}r^{2}}\vec{u_r}$$

When $2a<r<\infty$
$$\vec{E} = 0$$
